Pacific Dataviz Challenge 2025

Overview

This project was developed as part of the Pacific Dataviz Challenge 2025 to visualize progress toward Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 14.5.1, which measures the proportion of marine Key Biodiversity Areas (KBAs) that are covered by protected areas and/or Other Effective Area-Based Conservation Measures (OECMs).The theme of this year’s challenge, “Blue Pacific 2050”, references the long-term regional strategy for sustainable ocean management and collaboration across the Pacific. The 2050 Strategy for the Blue Pacific Continent is guided by the deep connection of Pacific peoples to their land and ocean.

Sustainable Development Goal 14 focuses on conserving and sustainably using the oceans, seas and marine resources. Indicator 14.5.1 tracks the percentage of marine areas that are under protection, a critical measure to preserve biodiversity, support climate resilience, and ensure sustainable livelihoods for coastal communities. This visualization explores how protected marine areas have evolved over time in the Pacific region.

Data Sources

  • Main dataset: Blue Pacific 2050: Ocean And Environment (Thematic Area 6), downloaded from the .Stat Data Explorer
  • Date of download: 30 July 2025
  • Geographic boundaries: GADM Level 0 (country level)

Indicator Focus

  • Indicator: ER_MRN_MARINKBA
  • SDG Code: 14.5.1
  • Description: Proportion of marine KBAs covered by protected areas or OECMs
  • Indicator Tier: Tier 1
  • Temporal Range: 2000–2019 (20 annual values per country)
  • Coverage: 14 Pacific countries (Niue and Tuvalu lack data for this indicator)

14.5.1 Coverage of protected areas in relation to marine areas (Tier I)

This indicator monitors the proportion of marine and coastal areas that are protected. It helps assess how well countries are safeguarding biodiversity and ecosystems in oceans and seas. The goal is to ensure sustainable use of marine resources by designating and maintaining effective marine protected areas.

Citation: Kraak MJ, RE Roth, B Ricker, A Kagawa, and G Le Sourd. 2020. Mapping for a Sustainable World. United Nations: New York, NY (USA).

Key Observations (2019)
